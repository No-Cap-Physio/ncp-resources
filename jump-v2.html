4 Things Your Rehab Skipped

You're back. But you're not back. Here's exactly why.

Standard physio gets your tissue healed. That's the job it was designed for. But basketball isn't walking or running in a straight line. It's full-speed cuts, contested jumps, reactive direction changes under fatigue. Nobody in your rehab pipeline was responsible for rebuilding those qualities. So they didn't.

The 4 Gaps Nobody Addressed
01
Your Power Was Never Rebuilt
Force-velocity gap
  • Strength and power are different qualities — your tissue healing doesn't restore your force-velocity profile
  • After injury and time off, your muscle's ability to produce force fast drops significantly — even when general strength comes back
  • This shows up in your EUR (Eccentric Utilization Ratio) — the ratio between your countermovement jump and squat jump
  • EUR below 1.10 means your stretch-shortening cycle isn't being used — you're jumping like someone with no elastic capacity
  • Most rehab programs stop at bodyweight squat. That's step one of about six.
What this looks like on court
"I feel slow off my first step. My pop just isn't there. I know what I want to do but my body isn't doing it fast enough."
NCP benchmark: CMJ >40cm male | EUR 1.10–1.25 | Broad jump >220cm
02
Deceleration Was Never Trained
The most dangerous gap
  • Most knee injuries don't happen on acceleration — they happen on deceleration and direction change
  • After injury, your body develops a lateral shift — your COM moves away from the injured side on every landing and cut
  • This means you're loading your hip and knee at bad angles on every deceleration rep
  • If nobody specifically trained this in rehab, you went back to the exact mechanics that were there when you got hurt
  • Sprint time can look fine but COD time is still slow — that gap is your deceleration deficit
What this looks like on court
"I hesitate before I cut hard. I slow myself down before I actually need to. I avoid certain defensive positions."
NCP benchmark: 5-10-5 COD <4.8s male | Sprint OK + COD slow = decel deficit
03
Your Elastic System Is Still Offline
Reactive strength gap
  • Basketball runs on your elastic system — tendons absorbing force and firing it straight back out
  • This quality shuts down after injury and does not come back just because you started playing again
  • RSI (Reactive Strength Index) is the number that tells you whether your elastic system is actually back online
  • RSI below 1.5 on a drop jump means you're in reactive deficit — your ground contact time is too long and you're losing energy on every landing
  • Fixing this requires specific plyometric progressions — not just jumping around in practice
What this looks like on court
"I'm always a step late. I used to react before I thought. Now I'm thinking before I move and that half second costs me everything."
NCP benchmark: RSI DJ >1.5 minimum | RSI Mod >0.9 | SL RSI >1.5 efficient
04
Your Movement Mechanics Were Never Restored
Biomechanics gap
  • After injury, your body locks into a compensation pattern — usually an anterior pelvic tilt, a lateral COM shift, or a rotation bias away from the injured side
  • This changes your hip's ability to internally rotate on your drive foot — which is the main mechanism for generating power off the cut and the first step
  • If your hip can't expand into internal rotation on the injured side, your knee takes the torque instead
  • This isn't about stretching your hip flexors — it's about restoring the respiratory-pelvic coupling that controls how your thorax and pelvis move relative to each other
  • Standard rehab doesn't address this. Most physios don't even assess it.
What this looks like on court
"My body feels stiff on one side. My drive is weaker going left vs right. I feel like I'm compensating but I don't know how to fix it."
NCP assessment: Hip IR symmetry within 10° bilateral | COM shift on SL landing

None of this is your fault. Standard physio wasn't built for this part of the journey. The gap between tissue healing and actual performance is a different program — and most rehab pipelines don't have one.
