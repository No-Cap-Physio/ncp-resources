Why You're Still Not Ready

Your clearance letter lied to you. Not on purpose. Here's what it actually means.

Your score isn't a failure — it's information. It means your body is telling you something your clearance letter didn't. The gap between medically cleared and performance ready is real. And it's exactly where most athletes get hurt a second time.

What Cleared Actually Means vs What You Think It Means
What you were told
"You're cleared. Tissue has healed. You're good to return."
What it actually means
Your tissue healed enough to tolerate activity. Your power output, mechanics, and trust have not been tested.
What you were told
"The scan looks clean. Everything checks out."
What it actually means
Structure is intact. A scan can't measure your RSI, your limb symmetry, your force-velocity profile, or your confidence under load.
What you were told
"Just ease back in. Listen to your body."
What it actually means
No criteria. No benchmarks. No progression plan. Listening to your body is not a return-to-performance protocol.
The 4 Stages Of A Real Return
Stage 1 — Tissue Tolerance
  • Can your body handle progressive loading without reacting?
  • Swelling, pain, or stiffness 24hrs after activity means you're still here
  • Regardless of what your clearance letter says
  • This is where most low scorers are sitting right now
Most common gap
Stage 2 — Strength and Power Symmetry
  • Both legs need to produce force and power within 10% of each other
  • Below that — LSI under 90% — your nervous system is compensating every single rep
  • Your COM shifts to the stronger side on every landing and cut
  • You won't feel it. But the data will show it
Often skipped
Stage 3 — Reactive and Elastic Capacity
  • Can your body absorb force and immediately re-express it?
  • This is your elastic system — the stretch-shortening cycle that makes you look athletic
  • RSI and EUR are the metrics that tell you if it's back online
  • It requires specific training to rebuild. It does not come back on its own
Rarely addressed
Stage 4 — Movement Restoration and Psych Readiness
  • Your compensation patterns need to be identified and addressed — not just exercised around
  • Hip IR mobility, COM control, pelvic orientation under load — these drive your risk profile
  • And your brain needs to catch up with your body through graded exposure to threatening movements
  • ACL-RSI below 65 is a significant re-injury risk factor — regardless of physical testing results
Final stage

Athletes who return before completing all four stages face significantly higher re-injury risk in the first 12 months back. Rushing back is not worth another 6-12 months on the sideline.
